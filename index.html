// how it works

From plaintext to protected in three steps

The same secure flow powers the AES Vault behind the scenes.

01

Derive a key

Your passphrase is salted and stretched through 250,000 rounds of PBKDF2 into a 256-bit AES key — slowing brute force to a crawl.

02

Encrypt & authenticate

AES-GCM seals your message with a random IV and produces an authentication tag, so any tampering is detected on decryption.

03

Share the blob

Salt, IV and ciphertext are packed into one Base64 string. Send it anywhere — only the passphrase can unlock it.
